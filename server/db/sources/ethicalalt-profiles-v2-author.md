# 01 — EXXONMOBIL CORPORATION

**brand_slug:** `exxonmobil`
**parent:** ExxonMobil Corporation (NYSE: XOM)
**subsidiaries:** Esso, Mobil, ExxonMobil Chemical, XTO Energy, Imperial Oil
**hq_country:** US
**founded:** 1999 (Standard Oil lineage to 1870)

## Tax Record
- Effective federal tax rate: frequently near zero or negative in years of high tax credit usage
- Significant offshore profit allocation through subsidiaries in Netherlands, Singapore, Switzerland
- Has paid negative US federal income tax in multiple recent years due to foreign tax credits and depletion allowances
- Good Jobs First: $6.1B in US state and federal subsidies documented
- **Tax concern level: SIGNIFICANT**

## Legal Record
- Climate knowledge suppression: Harvard researchers and Columbia journalism investigation (2015, 2017) documented that ExxonMobil's own scientists established the link between fossil fuels and climate change by the 1970s. Internal documents show the company then spent $30M+ funding climate denial think tanks and campaigns from 1990s–2000s while publicly questioning the science.
- State AG investigations: multiple state attorneys general (New York, Massachusetts, others) have investigated ExxonMobil for fraud related to misleading investors and the public about climate risk. New York case dismissed 2019; Massachusetts case ongoing.
- 1989 Exxon Valdez: 11 million gallons of crude oil spilled in Prince William Sound, Alaska. $507M punitive damages after years of litigation (reduced from $5B jury verdict by Supreme Court).
- 2010: ExxonMobil pipeline spill in Mayflower, Arkansas — $5M EPA settlement
- 2016: ExxonMobil agreed to $300M settlement re: refinery air pollution violations
- Multiple Superfund site connections
- 2024: ExxonMobil sued a small activist investor (Arjuna Capital) for filing a shareholder climate resolution — widely criticized as intimidation tactic against shareholder democracy
- **Legal concern level: SIGNIFICANT**

## Labor Record
- Standard energy industry practices in developed markets
- Refinery workers: documented exposure concerns, historical benzene exposure cases
- Downstream: significant contractor workforce with varying standards
- **Labor concern level: MINOR**

## Environmental Record
- Primary product: extraction, refining, and sale of fossil fuels — the primary driver of anthropogenic climate change
- Valdez: see above
- Methane emissions: ExxonMobil operations are significant methane emitters; methane is 80x more potent than CO2 over 20-year horizon
- Offshore drilling: operations in sensitive ecosystems including Arctic
- Plastic: ExxonMobil Chemical is a major producer of single-use plastic precursors
- CDP Climate: C (2023) — among the lowest of any major corporation
- Climate pledge: ExxonMobil has committed to net zero in its operated assets by 2050 — does not cover scope 3 emissions (the burning of its products, which is the majority of its climate impact)
- **Environmental concern level: SIGNIFICANT**

## Political
- Lobbying: $12.4M (2023) — among the largest US corporate lobbying operations
- Historically funded: Heritage Foundation, Heartland Institute, Competitive Enterprise Institute, American Legislative Exchange Council (ALEC) — all have published climate-skeptic material
- After internal documents were published: ExxonMobil shifted to carbon tax advocacy (opposed by the same groups it previously funded)
- API (American Petroleum Institute) membership: funded industry trade group lobbying

## Executive Web
- Darren Woods (CEO): no documented personal corruption
- Rex Tillerson (former CEO, Secretary of State 2017-2018): departed ExxonMobil, served in Trump administration. No criminal charges.
- Lee Raymond (former CEO): led the climate denial campaign era. Retired $400M compensation package.

## Product Health
- Fossil fuel combustion: causal link to air pollution (NOx, particulates) and climate change is established scientific consensus
- Benzene: ExxonMobil refineries are documented benzene emitters. Benzene is a known carcinogen with no safe exposure level.
- PFAS: ExxonMobil Chemical produces PFAS precursors used in industrial applications

## Summary
ExxonMobil's documented record centers on one of the most significant corporate scandals of the 21st century: internal company research established the climate science by the 1970s, and the company subsequently spent decades funding denial campaigns while publicly questioning that science. This is documented in company internal documents obtained through litigation and journalism. The company has also faced multiple environmental enforcement actions, paid effectively zero federal income tax in multiple years, and in 2024 sued a shareholder for filing a climate resolution.

# 02 — TYSON FOODS INC.

**brand_slug:** `tyson-foods`
**parent:** Tyson Foods Inc. (NYSE: TSN)
**subsidiaries:** Jimmy Dean, Ball Park, State Fair, Hillshire Farm, Sara Lee, Aidells, Wright Brand, ibp
**hq_country:** US
**founded:** 1935

## Tax Record
- Effective tax rate: approximately 23–25%
- Standard US food company structure
- Good Jobs First: $139M+ in documented government subsidies
- **Tax concern level: MINOR**

## Legal Record
- COVID-19, Waterloo Iowa (2020): Tyson Foods managers organized a betting pool on how many Waterloo plant workers would contract COVID-19. Multiple managers fired. Plant had one of the largest early outbreaks, with 1,031 workers infected and six dead. Tyson obtained court orders protecting employee identities while petitioning courts; local sheriff accused Tyson of lying to health officials to keep the plant open.
- Price-fixing: Tyson settled for $221.5M (2022) in a class action alleging it and other chicken processors fixed prices for broiler chickens. Additional antitrust cases in beef and pork are active.
- Water pollution: Tyson is consistently among the top US water polluters. Environment America documented Tyson dumped more toxic pollution into US waterways than any other company from 2010-2014. Violations documented in Arkansas, Kansas, Nebraska, and other states.
- Worker injuries: processing plant injury rates above industry average. OSHA citations documented across multiple facilities.
- 2020: multiple COVID-related wrongful death suits filed by families of deceased workers
- Nutrient pollution: Tyson's agricultural supply chain (corn, soy, animal waste) is a major contributor to Gulf of Mexico hypoxic dead zone
- **Legal concern level: SIGNIFICANT**

## Labor Record
- Processing plant workers: among the highest-injury manufacturing jobs in the US. Line speeds have been repeatedly cited as injury drivers.
- OSHA: numerous citations for amputation hazards, chemical exposure, and ergonomic injuries
- COVID handling: see above — widely documented as prioritizing production over worker safety
- Meatpacking workforce: heavily immigrant and refugee workforce with limited English, documented exploitation vulnerabilities
- No living wage certification
- Turned to automation investment post-COVID: stated goal to reduce headcount
- **Labor concern level: SIGNIFICANT**

## Environmental Record
- Water: documented as one of the largest industrial water polluters in the US
- CAFO supply chain: concentrated animal feeding operations associated with significant water and air pollution, antibiotic resistance
- Land use: Tyson's supply chain drives significant corn and soy cultivation — contributing to soil depletion, chemical runoff
- Antibiotic use: Tyson committed to reducing antibiotic use in chicken (some progress) but beef and pork supply chains continue significant antibiotic usage
- CDP Climate: D (2023) — among the lowest-rated food companies
- **Environmental concern level: SIGNIFICANT**

## Political
- Lobbying: $3.8M (2023) — focused on food safety regulation, line speed limits, CAFO rules
- Opposes line speed regulations that would reduce worker injury rates
- Active in fighting labeling requirements for country of origin

## Product Health
- Processed meats (Jimmy Dean, Ball Park, Sara Lee): high sodium, saturated fat, nitrates/nitrites
- WHO classified processed meat as Group 1 carcinogen (colon cancer) — same category as tobacco
- Recalls: multiple product recalls for listeria, salmonella, E. coli contamination across Tyson brands

## Summary
Tyson Foods' documented record is significant across multiple dimensions: the COVID-19 betting pool scandal in which managers gambled on worker infections while allegedly misrepresenting the situation to health officials; documented status as one of the largest industrial water polluters in the US; antitrust settlements for chicken price-fixing; and consistently high worker injury rates. The company produces predominantly ultra-processed meat products, two categories of which (processed meat) are classified as Group 1 carcinogens by WHO.

# 03 — BAYER AG (MONSANTO)

**brand_slug:** `bayer-monsanto`
**parent:** Bayer AG (ETR: BAYN)
**subsidiaries (via Monsanto acquisition 2018):** Roundup, RoundupReady seed lines, Dekalb, Asgrow, Channel, Climate Corporation, Seminis, De Ruiter
**other Bayer brands:** Aspirin, Aleve, Claritin, One-A-Day, Coppertone, Dr. Scholl's, Canesten, Alka-Seltzer, Midol
**hq_country:** Germany
**founded:** 1863 (Bayer) / 1901 (Monsanto)

## Tax Record
- Bayer: German corporate tax approximately 24-28%
- Monsanto legacy structures in US: offshore IP licensing used
- Standard pharmaceutical/agri tax optimization
- **Tax concern level: MINOR to MODERATE**

## Legal Record
- Roundup / Glyphosate: Bayer inherited Monsanto's glyphosate liability when it acquired the company for $63B in 2018. As of 2024, Bayer has paid approximately $11B+ to settle Roundup cancer claims (non-Hodgkin lymphoma). Over 170,000 cases were filed. The International Agency for Research on Cancer (IARC) classified glyphosate as "probably carcinogenic" (Group 2A) in 2015 — a finding Monsanto/Bayer has contested.
- Roundup suppression: internal Monsanto documents obtained in litigation (the "Monsanto Papers") showed the company worked to ghostwrite scientific papers defending glyphosate, manipulate regulatory processes, and undermine independent scientists. Documents revealed strategy labeled "Let Nothing Go" to discredit critics.
- PCBs (Monsanto legacy): Monsanto was the primary US manufacturer of polychlorinated biphenyls (PCBs) from 1935-1977. PCBs are now classified as probable human carcinogens and persist in the environment. They contaminated hundreds of Superfund sites. Monsanto internal documents showed the company knew of health risks and continued production. $550M settlement with Anniston, Alabama (2003) over PCB contamination.
- Agent Orange (Monsanto legacy): Monsanto was a primary manufacturer of Agent Orange herbicide/defoliant used in Vietnam War. Exposure linked to cancer and birth defects. $180M settlement with US veterans (1984).
- Seed monopoly: Monsanto/Bayer controls approximately 25-30% of global seed market. Farmers are prohibited from saving seeds for next planting — must repurchase annually. Multiple competition investigations in EU, US, India.
- Dicamba: Bayer's dicamba herbicide (system requiring RoundupReady seeds) has caused documented drift damage to neighboring farms not using the herbicide. $400M jury verdict against Bayer (2020) in Missouri case. Multiple regulatory restrictions imposed.
- Bayer Essure: medical device causing serious complications — removed from market 2019 after FDA action. $1.6B settlement.
- Bayer blood thinner (Xarelto): $775M settlement re: inadequate bleeding risk disclosure.
- **Legal concern level: SIGNIFICANT**

## Labor Record
- German headquarters: strong labor protections, works council representation
- Agricultural supply chain: farmworker exposure to glyphosate is primary documented concern
- **Labor concern level: MINOR**

## Environmental Record
- Glyphosate: see above — IARC Group 2A carcinogen, widespread environmental persistence
- PCB contamination: Monsanto's legacy PCB pollution affects hundreds of sites globally
- GMO monoculture: RoundupReady crop systems have contributed to reduced crop biodiversity and "superweeds" resistant to glyphosate
- Neonicotinoids: Bayer is a primary manufacturer of neonicotinoid insecticides linked to bee colony collapse
- **Environmental concern level: SIGNIFICANT**

## Political
- Lobbying: $7.2M (2023, US) plus significant EU lobbying
- USDA/FDA revolving door: documented movement of former Monsanto executives and lawyers into regulatory positions
- IARC controversy: after IARC's glyphosate classification, Monsanto/Bayer worked to discredit the agency and influence the subsequent EPA review process — documented in litigation discovery

## Product Health
- Roundup: WHO IARC "probably carcinogenic." $11B+ in cancer settlements.
- Aspirin, Claritin, Aleve: standard OTC drug profiles
- Coppertone: PFAS detected in some sunscreen formulations (third-party testing)

## Summary
Bayer/Monsanto's documented record is among the most severe in this database. The Monsanto Papers — internal documents obtained through Roundup litigation — show a systematic campaign to suppress health research, ghostwrite favorable scientific papers, and undermine regulatory processes. The company's legacy PCB contamination affected hundreds of Superfund sites. $11B+ in glyphosate cancer settlements with 170,000+ cases. The genetic seed monopoly raises separate concerns about food sovereignty. When Bayer paid $63B for Monsanto, it acquired all of these liabilities — a decision that erased nearly all of Bayer's market cap within two years.

**overall_concern_level:** `significant`

- Project Dragonfly: Google began building a censored search engine for China (2017-2018). Abandoned after employee protest and Congressional scrutiny. No legal resolution.
- NLRB: Google fired employees involved in political organizing in 2019. NLRB settled 2021.
- Street View: collected WiFi payload data (including emails, passwords) from home networks while photographing streets in 30+ countries. Settled globally.

## Labor Record
- 2018 Google walkout: 20,000 employees worldwide walked out over sexual harassment handling, specifically the departure deal for Andy Rubin (Android creator) who received $90M exit package despite substantiated sexual harassment claim

- "Shadow" workforce: Google employs more contractors than full employees — contractors lack benefits and protections
- **Labor concern level: MODERATE**

## Environmental Record
- Data centers: among the largest corporate energy consumers globally. Committed to carbon-free energy 24/7 by 2030.
- CDP Climate: A (2023) — more credible climate commitment than most tech companies
- AI energy: Google's AI products are significantly increasing energy consumption
- **Environmental concern level: MINOR (improving)**

## Political
- Lobbying: $12.5M (2023) — antitrust, AI regulation, privacy law, copyright
- Has cultivated relationships across administrations; significant revolving door with government
- EU: Google has faced multiple EU competition investigations and billions in fines

## Product Health
- Android/Pixel: standard consumer electronics
- YouTube: documented algorithmic radicalization pathway — research shows YouTube recommendations can lead users from mainstream content toward extremist content
- No physical product health concerns

## Summary
Google's documented record centers on two things: the most significant monopoly ruling in decades (paying $20B/year to maintain default search status, found by a federal judge to be illegal monopolization), and decades of aggressive tax avoidance through the Double Irish Dutch Sandwich structure. Secondary: $170M COPPA violation for collecting children's data, $391M location tracking settlement, and firing workers who organized against sexual harassment handling.
